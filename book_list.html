Wings of Fire | A P J Abdul Kalam, Arun Tiwari

This is the story of a man who rose from obscurity, and his pursuit of learning science, failures, success, and unparalleled career as a defense scientist, as well as India's story of becoming one of the major ... Read More players in the space and defense field.

The boy of Rameshwaram who was eager to learn as much as possible about science attended MIT, later worked for HAL where he was hired for ISRO by Satish Dhawan and Vikram Sarabhai. He helped to launch the SLV rocket in space. Later he worked for DRDO and developed Agni, Prithvi, Nag, and many other missiles.

The book is simple and inspiring. The success doesn't come only by passion; luck and chances are also very essential for success. When we talk about success then passion, hard work, and talent overshadow luck, chance, opportunity, and money. But there are many stories in the book where Kalam Shahab get chances and opportunities. Abdul Kalam was lucky that Satish Dhawan found him at HAL and after the unsuccessful launch of SLV in 1979, he was given another chance to led the project. He was given the opportunity to speak in parliament by Satish Dhawan. There is no doubt that Kalam Shahab was a genius. Unlike a self-help book, he didn't fail to mention other factors that made him one of the great and popular scientists of India. This single thing makes this book a truly inspiring book.

If you want to have a glimpse of India's journey into space and defense or want to read a true inspiring book then you can read this book.   Read Less
